The Situation

You work harder every year.
The numbers don't always follow.

The five things that keep every TPO up at night, and why traditional solutions have failed to fix them.

01
"I have no idea which students are behind until it's too late."
There is no early warning. You find out a student is struggling when they fail their first GD, sometimes after a recruiter has already eliminated them. By then, the semester is almost over.
02
"Students freeze in group discussions. Every single year."
They know the topic. They just cannot hold their ground when eight voices speak at once. They have never practiced under that kind of pressure. One seminar per semester is not practice, it is attendance.
03
"Recruiters tell us the resumes all look the same."
Every student writes 'Python: Intermediate.' 'Communication: Strong.' No proof. No differentiation. Hiring managers have stopped trusting what they read, and your students pay the price.
04
"Students don't know what they actually want to do."
They choose job vs. MBA vs. startup in final year, under panic. Unclear students give vague interviews. Vague interviews end placements. The confusion starts in Year 1, but no one addresses it until final year.
05
"I cannot build a NAAC accreditation case without proper placement data."
Manual tracking in Excel, anecdotal outcomes, no digital audit trail. Accreditation bodies want structured, verifiable placement data, aligned to Criterion 5.2 and 5.3. Most TPOs spend weekends compiling it by hand.
"The placement problem is a first-year problem
dressed as a final-year emergency."
