The Reality

You study for four years.
But nobody taught you how to get placed.

The five reasons most engineering students miss placements, and why watching YouTube videos and attending one GD seminar will never fix them.

01
"I know what to say in a GD. I just freeze when eight people are talking at once."
Knowing theory is not the same as handling pressure. You've never been in a real GD simulation, with interruptions, dominant voices, and 90 seconds to make your point heard. That's not a knowledge gap. It's a practice gap.
02
"I still don't know whether I want a job, an MBA, or something else entirely."
You're not lazy. You're confused, and nobody around you has given you a way to actually experience each path before committing. Unclear students give vague interviews. Vague interviews end in rejections. The confusion starts in Year 1 but hits you in Final Year.
03
"My resume says 'Python: Intermediate', just like everyone else's."
Recruiters have stopped trusting self-reported skills. Every student claims the same things. There's no way to stand out unless you can prove what you know. Not just list it. You pay the price for a system that rewards claims over demonstrations.
04
"My English is okay for studies but I go blank when I have to speak professionally."
You have the ideas. You have the knowledge. But "Tell me about yourself" makes you switch to survival mode. A cold email to a recruiter takes you 40 minutes to draft. This is not lack of intelligence. It's lack of the right practice environment.
05
"I'm already in 3rd year and I haven't done anything to prepare for placements."
Most students start thinking about placements in final year, and that is exactly 2–3 years too late. GD confidence, communication skills, career clarity, and a verified skill profile take time to build. The students who get placed in the first campus drive started well before placement season began.
"Placement day is not the problem.
The three years before it are."

01 - GD Coach
Never Freeze in a GD Again
GD Coach simulates a live group discussion with multiple AI participants who interrupt, challenge, and dominate. Exactly like the real thing. Practice any time, unlimited sessions, and get a score after every round so you can see yourself improve.

02 - VoiceUp & ProWrite
Kill the Language Barrier Before It Kills Your Offer
VoiceUp gives you 60-second rehearsal environments for the moments that terrify most students: "Tell me about yourself," "Pitch your idea," "Ask a question to the panel." ProWrite transforms broken English into professional communication instantly. No embarrassment, no judgment.
Language stops being the ceiling between you and your offer.
🏅
03 - 3-Level Skill Verification
Prove Your Skills. Stop Just Claiming Them.
Level 1: Upload your certificate. Level 2: Demonstrate the skill live in a monitored environment. Level 3: Get endorsed by an industry professional who works in that domain. Every verified skill becomes a badge that recruiters can independently audit, before you walk in the door.
You compete on what you can actually do, not just what you've written on paper.

Your Profile That Proves More Than a PDF Ever Could
Your EduPodX profile shows recruiters everything a static resume can't: GD confidence scores, verified skill badges, career clarity indicators, practice consistency, and mentor endorsements. It's LinkedIn + Resume + Proof, combined in one place.
Recruiters who see a Living Resume shortlist 60% faster. It removes the guesswork.
